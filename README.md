# Supply Chain Performance & Risk Analysis 📊

## 📌 Executive Summary
[cite_start]This project provides a data-driven analysis of supply chain operations, covering **$25.3M in total sales** [cite: 7, 8] [cite_start]and **504.3K units ordered**[cite: 9, 10]. The analysis focuses on evaluating supplier reliability, identifying logistical bottlenecks, and mitigating supply chain disruptions.

## 🚀 Strategic Insights (Based on Data)

* [cite_start]**Financial Performance:** The **Electronics** category is the leading revenue driver, contributing **$5.69M** to total sales[cite: 23].
* [cite_start]**Supplier Risk Profile:** A significant portion of operations is under threat, with **51.4% (514 orders)** flagged as **"At Risk"**[cite: 28, 56, 57]. 
* [cite_start]**Reliability Metrics:** The average supplier reliability score is **0.7**[cite: 53], indicating a need for improved supplier vetting.
* [cite_start]**Operational Bottlenecks:** While the overall average delay is **1.9 days** [cite: 11, 12][cite_start], a critical spike occurs in **December**, where delays jump to **4.2 days**[cite: 128, 133].
* [cite_start]**Disruption Drivers:** The most frequent causes of disruptions are **Shortages** and **Weather conditions**[cite: 46, 47, 75, 77].
* [cite_start]**Logistics Efficiency:** **Air shipping** is the most reliable mode with the lowest delays (approx **1.75 days**) [cite: 90, 109][cite_start], whereas **Sea and Rail** shipping modes experience the highest average delays (over **2.0 days**)[cite: 107, 110].

## 🛠 Tech Stack & Methodology
* [cite_start]**Tool:** Power BI / Data Visualization[cite: 45].
* [cite_start]**Key Metrics:** Order Value, Supplier Reliability Score, Supply Risk Flag, and Disruption Severity[cite: 13, 23, 45, 63].
