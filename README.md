# 📦 Supply Chain Performance & Risk Analysis

## 📌 Executive Summary
This project provides a data-driven analysis of supply chain operations, covering **$25.3M in total sales** and **504.3K units ordered**. The analysis focuses on evaluating supplier reliability, identifying logistical bottlenecks, and mitigating supply chain disruptions.

---

## 🛠 Tech Stack & Methodology
To ensure data accuracy and professional visualization, I followed these steps:
* **Excel:** Used for Data Cleaning and preprocessing (handling missing values, formatting, and preparing the dataset).
* **Power BI:** Used for Data Modeling and creating interactive dashboards to visualize key metrics (KPIs).

---

## ⚠️ The Problem (Business Challenge)
The supply chain was facing unpredictable delays and high risks without a clear understanding of the root causes. Management needed to identify:
* Why certain months (like December) have high delays despite lower order volumes.
* Which suppliers are unreliable and causing potential shortages.
* How shipping modes affect the overall delivery timeline.

---

## 🚀 Strategic Insights (Key Findings)
* **Inefficient Planning:** The highest cause of disruption is **"Shortages"**, followed by weather, meaning the issue is more about inventory planning than transportation.
* **Reliability Gap:** Over **51% of orders** are handled by suppliers with a low reliability score (**0.7**), creating a massive vulnerability.
* **The December Bottleneck:** Data shows a spike in delays to **4.2 days** in December, indicating a local logistics or warehousing bottleneck.
* **Financial Driver:** The **Electronics** category is the leading revenue driver, contributing **$5.69M** to total sales.

---

## 💡 Recommendations (Action Plan)
* **Supplier Audit:** Immediate re-evaluation of the **514 "High Risk" suppliers**. Shift volume toward suppliers with reliability scores above 0.85.
* **Proactive Inventory:** Increase safety stock levels for the **Electronics category** to mitigate frequent "Shortage" disruptions.
* **December Buffer:** Implement a "Pre-December" shipping strategy to move goods in November and avoid the 4.2-day delay peak.
* **Mode Optimization:** Shift critical orders to **Air shipping** during peak disruption seasons to maintain customer satisfaction.
